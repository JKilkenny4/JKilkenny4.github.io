Who knew building a website required so much talking about oneself?

I'm a data-phile based in Philadelphia always looking to explore new ideas, technologies, and projects. I received a M.S. in Experimental Psychology from Saint Joseph's University in 2019. My thesis research explored how college-aged offspring who grew up with helicopter parents coped with stress.

While at Saint Joe's, I also conducted research into traumatic brain injuries, the effects of early life and adolescent stress, emotion regulation, and cognitive responses to stress. It's also where I developed a passion for statistics and data science. Advanced research methods and statistics were my favorite classess by far, and I was privileged enough to be able to work as a TA for both undergraduate and graduate statistics courses.

After graduating, I began working in market research consulting for various pharmaceutical and bio-tech clients. My work spans both quantitative and qualitative research depending on the needs of the project, but I specialize in large-scale longitudinal tracking studies. I have experience in both patient and HCP research, and I have worked in a number of different therapeutic areas including oncology, neurology, and rheumatology, among others.

Much of my free time is spent coding and work on side projects. I am confident coding / working in Python, R, SQL, and SPSS, comfortable using project management tools such as Git and Docker, and experienced building dashboards in Looker. I enjoy reading about new statistical and ML approaches, and I'm currently interested in exploring unsupervised and reinforcement learning more. I've been slowly reading through Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction by Sutton and Barto over the last few weeks, so I'm especially looking forward to building some Monte Carlo simulations in the future.

I'm also an avid golfer, which tends to occupy the rest of my free time, for better or worse.
